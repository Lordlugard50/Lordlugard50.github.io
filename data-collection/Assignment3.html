Map: Median Household Income in Texas

The choropleth map shows that median household income in Texas varies sharply across counties. Metropolitan areas such as Collin, Travis, and Fort Bend counties report the highest incomes, reflecting urban job markets and economic opportunities. In contrast, many rural and border counties, especially in South Texas, display much lower household incomes. This pattern underscores the economic divide between prosperous metro regions and struggling rural communities.

The poverty rate rankings highlight a clear divide. Counties like Dimmit (44.8%), Zapata (37.4%), and Presidio (36.3%) are among the highest, with poverty concentrated in smaller, rural, and border areas. By contrast, counties such as Rockwall (4.3%) and Collin (6.3%) have the lowest poverty rates, reflecting their affluent suburban economies. While the ACS provides strong insight, large margins of error in smaller counties (e.g., Kenedy County) mean results should be interpreted with caution.
