About Me

A brief overview of my academic path, research interests, and skills.

Academic Journey

I am a PhD Candidate in Public Policy & Political Economy and an M.Sc. Candidate in Data Analytics at The University of Texas at Dallas. My work sits at the intersection of political economy, development, and applied data science.

I am particularly interested in topics such as structural adjustment programs, resource-backed loans, African development, and labor-market outcomes in the United States.

Methods & Tools

I work extensively with R, Python, and ArcGIS for statistical analysis, machine learning, survival analysis, spatial analytics, and data visualization.

R · Tidyverse Machine Learning GIS / ArcGIS Text Analytics Quantitative Methods

Current Projects

My portfolio includes projects on violent crime in U.S. states, macroeconomic policy reforms, AI and jobs, and African development finance. I also build interactive dashboards and experiences to make complex results accessible.
